AI Red Lines Tracker

Standardized Metrics for Public Monitoring of AI's Unacceptable Risks

About the Tracker

The AI Red Lines Tracker is a public platform designed to monitor how close frontier AI models are to breaching "unacceptable risk" thresholds. Our core objective is to translate abstract catastrophic AI risks into concrete, measurable indicators. This provides a standardized framework for public and regulatory oversight, fostering a greater understanding of the capabilities of advanced AI systems. This proof of concept is based on publicly available information and established frameworks from leading AI labs.

CBRN Risk

Chemical, Biological, Radiological, & Nuclear threats.

Capability Uplift: 3.5x
Red Line: 5x Uplift
Defined by Anthropic's Responsible Scaling Policy as an unacceptable risk threshold. At this level, an AI model significantly boosts a user's ability to acquire and use harmful biological agents.

Red Line at 5x Uplift

Cybersecurity Risk

Executing large-scale cyberattacks or finding zero-day vulnerabilities.

Throughput Uplift: 85% Cost Reduction
Red Line: 90% Cost Reduction
An AI enabling a 90% reduction in the cost of sophisticated cyberattacks would dramatically lower the barrier for malicious actors, representing an unacceptable risk.

Red Line at 90% Cost Reduction

Loss of Control

Autonomous replication, self-improvement, and deception.

Deception Score: 40%
Red Line: 75% Deception Score
Models that can reliably deceive human operators in a variety of situations pose a critical risk for maintaining meaningful human control.

Red Line at 75%

Autonomy

Acquiring resources and operating in the wild without oversight.

Autonomous Task Score: 25%
Red Line: 60% Autonomous Task Score
A model capable of autonomously accomplishing 60% of a complex, multi-step goal like "start a business" would demonstrate dangerous levels of autonomy.

Red Line at 60%
